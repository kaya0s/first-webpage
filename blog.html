WHAT I HAVE LEARNED IN INTRO TO COMPUTING




My journey into the exciting world of computing has been rewarding and enlightening. From basic hardware and software to complex programming languages, each course has demonstrated a deep understanding of the technological landscape that shapes our modern world

An examination of the history of computing revealed remarkable developments in the field. Pioneers with sheer ingenuity and dedication that inspired awe as they witnessed the journey from the first generation of large indoor filling machines to the sleek, powerful machines we use today paved the way for the technological revolution that has taken place in every aspect our lives.

The diversity of professionals in the IT sector made us aware of the great career opportunities offered in this industry. From software engineers developing the complex algorithms that power our everyday applications to cybersecurity experts protecting our digital lives, every role contributes to the growth and security of the digital world

The ever-present threat of cyber-attacks served as a stark reminder of weaknesses in the digital profession. Learning about various types of cybercrimes, from phishing scams to malware attacks, equipped me with the knowledge to navigate the online world with caution and knowledge. Highlighting the vital role that cybersecurity plays in protecting ourselves, and our data, in the digital age, they highlighted the importance of implementing strong security measures and seizing the internet so safe practices accept it.

Also delving into HTML and CSS basics opened doors for web development. Understanding the basic architecture of web pages provided the foundation for creating and designing quality online experiences.

The portion of learning that I find most challenging





It felt like a maze in darkness trying to learn HTML and CSS from scratch.

My frustration reached its peak with CSS. The cascading nature of the styles, with rules overriding each other in an intricate dance, was a constant source of headaches. Debugging layout issues was like trying to untangle a Gordian knot, and the sheer volume of properties and values felt impossible to grasp. I'd spend countless hours tweaking and adjusting styles, only to end up with unexpected results and a growing sense of despair.
